01 — About

A bit about me

I'm currently pursuing my M.Eng. in Mechanical Engineering at Cornell, after completing dual B.S. degrees in Biomedical and Mechanical Engineering at Penn State. My passion lies in the space where quality systems, process engineering, and hands-on validation come together!

During my time at Fresenius Kabi, I worked on 510(k) submissions, investigated device complaints, and helped refine technical drawings to meet FDA and ASME standards. One of my favorite parts of the role was designing an automated test setup that turned what used to be a 28-hour manual process into something that could run overnight. Experiences like that are what drew me deeper into engineering—taking a problem and finding a smarter, more efficient way to solve it.

I've worked within ISO 13485 and ISO 9001 environments, and I try to bring that same structured, detail-oriented mindset into everything I do. At the same time, I enjoy building and experimenting—whether that's prototyping, coding, or working on new ideas from scratch. Working in a medical device industry truly opened my eyes in what it means to adhere to compliance and it further deepens my appreciation for engineering and quality!

I'm currently based in Ithaca, NY and open to opportunities where I can continue learning, building, and contributing to meaningful engineering work. I'm especially interested in eventually building and leading engineering-driven products from the ground up.
