A bit about me

FIG. 01 — THE ENGINEERAS BUILT

I recently completed my M.Eng. in Mechanical Engineering at Cornell (GPA 3.95), after earning dual B.S. degrees in Biomedical and Mechanical Engineering at Penn State. My work lives where quality systems, process engineering, and hands-on validation come together.

At Fresenius Kabi, I supported Class II 510(k) submissions, investigated device complaints through CAPA, and revised 40+ technical drawings to FDA and ASME standards. My favorite part of the role: designing an automated durability tester that turned a 28-hour manual process into something that runs overnight — and reengineering validation workflows to cut costs by 80%. Taking a problem and finding a smarter, repeatable way to solve it is what drew me deeper into engineering.

I've worked inside ISO 13485 and ISO 9001 environments, and I bring that same structured, detail-oriented mindset to everything I build — whether that's a fail-safe system for an industrial robot, a designed experiment with 200+ runs, or an FPV drone on my workbench. Working in medical devices showed me what real compliance demands, and it deepened my appreciation for engineering done right.

I'm based in Ithaca, NY, open to relocation, and looking for quality, manufacturing, process, or R&D engineering roles in the medical device industry. Long term, I want to build and lead engineering-driven products from the ground up.
